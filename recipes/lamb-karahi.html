Lamb Karahi

Description

Lamb Karahi is a traditional Pakistani dish known for its rich and spicy flavors. Made with tender lamb cooked in a wok-like karahi with tomatoes, ginger, garlic, and aromatic spices, this dish is perfect for a hearty meal served with naan or rice.

Ingredients

  • 1 pound lamb, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 2 large tomatoes, chopped
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon ginger, minced
  • 2 green chilies, chopped
  • ½ cup plain yogurt
  • ¼ cup cooking oil
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
  • ½ teaspoon turmeric powder
  • ½ teaspoon red chili powder
  • ½ teaspoon garam masala
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh cilantro, chopped, for garnish
  • Fresh ginger, julienned, for garnish

Steps

  1. Heat the cooking oil in a large karahi or wok over medium-high heat. Add cumin seeds and let them sizzle for a few seconds.
  2. Add the chopped onions and saut√© until golden brown. Add the minced garlic and ginger, and cook for another minute.
  3. Add the lamb pieces to the karahi and cook until browned on all sides.
  4. Stir in the chopped tomatoes, green chilies, coriander powder, turmeric powder, red chili powder, and salt. Cook until the tomatoes are soft and the spices are well combined.
  5. Add the plain yogurt and mix well. Reduce the heat to low, cover the karahi, and let the lamb cook for 30-40 minutes, or until tender, stirring occasionally.
  6. Once the lamb is cooked, sprinkle garam masala over the top and mix well.
  7. Garnish with fresh cilantro and julienned ginger before serving. Enjoy with naan or steamed rice.
