Biryani

Description

Biryani is a fragrant and flavorful South Asian dish made with basmati rice, tender meat, and a blend of spices. This one-pot meal is perfect for special occasions and is sure to impress with its rich taste and aromatic presentation.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups basmati rice
  • 1 pound chicken, lamb, or beef, cut into pieces
  • 1 large onion, thinly sliced
  • 2 tomatoes, chopped
  • 1 cup plain yogurt
  • 2 tablespoons ghee or oil
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon ginger, minced
  • 2 green chilies, chopped
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
  • ½ teaspoon turmeric powder
  • ½ teaspoon red chili powder
  • ½ teaspoon garam masala
  • 4 cups water or broth
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh cilantro and mint leaves, chopped, for garnish
  • Fried onions, for garnish

Steps

  1. Rinse the basmati rice under cold water until the water runs clear. Soak the rice in water for 30 minutes, then drain.
  2. Heat ghee or oil in a large pot over medium heat. Add cumin seeds and let them sizzle for a few seconds.
  3. Add the sliced onions and saut√© until golden brown. Add garlic, ginger, and green chilies, and cook for another minute.
  4. Add the meat and cook until browned on all sides. Stir in the chopped tomatoes and cook until they soften.
  5. Mix in the yogurt, coriander powder, turmeric powder, red chili powder, garam masala, and salt. Cook for a few minutes until the spices are well combined with the meat.
  6. Add the soaked and drained rice to the pot, stirring gently to combine with the meat and spices.
  7. Pour in the water or broth and bring to a boil. Reduce the heat to low, cover the pot, and simmer for 20-25 minutes, or until the rice is fully cooked and the liquid is absorbed.
  8. Remove from heat and let the biryani sit, covered, for 10 minutes. Fluff the rice with a fork and garnish with fresh cilantro, mint leaves, and fried onions before serving.
